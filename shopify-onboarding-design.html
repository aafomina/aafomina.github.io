Shopify Customer Onboarding

Analysis

The Onboarding Process

Getting a customer to sign up is only half the battle. When they finally open a product there is plenty of time for customers to get confused, not find the help they need, spend too much time on tasks that are suppose to be simple and ultimately leave. A solid on-boarding process focuses on providing a successful experience to customer from the time they sign up as a trial user to the time they subscribe as a paying customer. The on-boarding process helps customers derive value from the product post-subscriptions decreasing the chance they may leave after paying one or two times.

An effective on boarding process is essential to:

  1. Decrease the immediate drop-offs that happen after a customer signs-up for a trial.
  2. Increasing trial to paid customer conversion rates.
  3. Decrease churn from customer groups that only subscribes for 30 to 90 days.

A well thought-out on-boarding process should guide the user through a series of success metrics.

The Shopify trial is 14 days. It is important to look at the tasks the most valuable users completed in the first 24 to 48 hours after signing up and model the process based on those success metrics.

For example:

  1. In 24 hours the user most likely to subscribe added 1 products to their store.
  2. In 48 hours the user most likely to subscribe added 5 products to their store.
  3. By the end of the trial, users that paid chose their theme and opened their store for business.

Questions to ask from data:

  1. What percentage of trial users actually complete each step in the journey below?
  2. How many users drop off and leave Shopify at each step? Which step has the most drop offs?
  3. How many steps are completed before a customer decides not to subscribe to Shopify?
  4. What range of tasks do users have completed when they sign up for a trial?
  5. What is the churn? How long does a customer keep their plan after the trial before cancelling?
  6. What steps does the customer has completed before unsubscribing? (looking only at customers 30 to 90 days after trial)
  7. Where does a customer struggle before unsubscribing in the 90 day period post conversion?

Questions to ask from user tests & customer support:

  1. What do customers complain most about during the 14 day trial?
  2. Where do customers get stuck when they first use the Shopify platform?
  3. Where do customers struggle before unsubscribing in the 90 day period post conversion?

eCommerce Goals

Below is an example of tasks a user needs to complete at each step to successfully set up and launch an eCommerce website. It’s important to note that every customer will have a different needs and a different approach to eCommerce. Further user testing is required to find the optimal process.

Step 1

Get an idea to open a store

  1. Brainstorm products to sell
  2. Read Shopify eCommerce guides
  3. Find ways to manufacture products
  4. Find ways to buy existing products

Step 2

Create an online store

  1. Sign-up for Shopify trial
  2. Log in and complete forms
  3. Name store

Step 3

Add products to store

  1. Add products manually
  2. Automatically import products
  3. Use suppliers app to import products
  4. Write title and description
  5. Add images
  6. Add pricing

Step 4

Customize design

  1. Choose a theme and colours
  2. Customize theme
  3. Hire someone to customize theme
  4. Add custom pages

Step 5

Add payment options

  1. Add address and choose currency
  2. Set up a payment account (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)

Step 6

Add branding & content

  1. Brainstorm domain names
  2. Buy a domain name
  3. Add domain name to Shopify
  4. Create and add a logo
  5. Add email capture
  6. Customize landing pages
  7. Create blog posts
  8. And a lot more...

Step 7

Open & promote

  1. Publish and view store
  2. Share with friends
  3. Set up social media accounts
  4. Use Shopify sales channels apps
  5. Reach out to media
  6. Hire marketing help
  7. And a lot more...

Step 8

Make first sale

  1. Set up automated emails
  2. Create and send invoice
  3. Ship product to customer
  4. Set up reporting and accounting

Redesign Goals

I will be redesigning the on-boarding process based on the principles below:

Transparent Progress

Show the use their learning journey in advance. Help user learn by doing instead of reading.

Simple & Small Tasks

Break down learning tasks. Get user to complete one success metric at a time. User should not have to think.

Seamless User Flow

Unify design elements. Split up forms and gather less info at each step. Make going from one step to another unnoticeable.

Personalized Experience

Ask users what their needs and challenges are. Provide personalized guides, videos and resources based on answers. Prompt support messages.

Connected Resources

Integrate all Shopify learning resources together and make them more visible in product - guides, tutorials, hire an expert, etc.

Help One Click Away

Make help links easy to find and live chat quickly available. Show users the right help at common points of frustration.

Perfectly Timed Value

Don’t overwhelm with too much information. Focus attention on the right features at the right time.

Rewarding & Exciting

Celebrate when users completes step (animations, messaging). Show user progress. Build in social sharing. Make it fun!

User tests

I did two user tests in 24 hours. I watched the customers go through the process of setting up a store. They spoke out loud about their frustrations and general experience with Shopify.

Nikita Kazakov
- Entrepreneur & Freelancer

Engineering graduate
Passive income enthusiast
Has programming skills
Likes trial & error and guides
Dropshipper

Story

Nikita creates custom merchandize and Shopify stores for YouTubers. He is also playing around with dropshipping businesses. He thinks Shopify is great to start a store but may move to Wordpress for more customizability.

Pain Points

  • The form asking for my address was unnecessary. I had to go into my payments settings to change my currency anyway.
  • I was still lost on the Home page even though the form asked too many questions from me already.
  • I clicked on every single link in the navigation panel to figure out how to set up my store.
  • To import products I used Oberlo. I heard about it from a friend and that is the only reason I used Shopify.
  • Finding how to change payment options and currency was confusing.
  • The customize theme page is a bit too much to start. I didn’t know how to add a new page or what Collection was.
  • I’m getting too many emails (like 2 a day) with videos and tutorials. Why can’t those be integrated into the platform instead?

Insights

  • It would be nice to know the work flow at the beginning and have an example of how people set-up stores.
  • There should be a guide pointing me to where I should start after signing-up.
  • Third party apps, especially for Drop shipping, should be suggested when signing-up.
  • I loved the Dropshipping guide. When I was building my store I had to exit to other parts of Shopify to find it again. It would be nice for the guides to be included while I set up.
  • I was excited to share my store with my friends. Can you make that easier?

Chris Labelle
- Co-founder & COO
Mosaic Manufacturing

Business graduate
Hardware entrepreneur
No programming skills
Likes videos & interactive tutorials
Time conscious

Story

Chris used Shopify to sell his 3D printing product but gave up on first try. He hired an expensive developer from Crew to build a customized store. Eventually the developer went back to design a custom theme for Chris using Shopify. Chris still has a love hate relationship with the blogging system (only part of Shopif he touches).

Pain Points

"I hate this screen [in reference to the Home screen after answering form questions and setting up store name]. I have no idea where to go. There's so many options. Do I make a theme? Do I add products? What do I need to do to get my store running? Where shold I go first? There's actually no direction at all once you get to this page, other than the button trying to get me to sign up for a plan (even though I haven't even done anything yet). There's too many options and im not far enough in the process to understand them."

Insights

  • If Chris was able to customize his theme quickly on his own, he would have signed-up as a paying customer faster.
  • If he had steps to guide him, he would have been less overwhlemed and knew exactly the time commitment required.
  • If a message told him about the Shopify marketplace he would have hired an expert, saving time and money.
