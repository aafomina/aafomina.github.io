ParseHub

ParseHub is a desktop app that makes web data extraction easy for anyone, even without technical skills. By using a graphic interfaced powered by machine learning, all you have to do is open a website, click on the web page and wait to download your data via JSON, CSV and API.

I co-founded ParseHub with 2 others in 2014 and lead product design, marketing, sales, customer support and business operations.

Results:

  • Bootstrapped (no outside funding) company from a basement to an office down-town Toronto with 12 employees.
  • Created SaaS inbound sales funnel with 20% m/m paid customer growth and 200 paying customers from companies like Walmart, Wiser, JP Morgan & Chase.
  • Grew website visitors by 425% in 1 year.
  • Increased sign-ups by 75% in 4 months to 70 users per day.
  • Doubled traffic to sign-ups conversion rate to 25% by testing different design and web copy.
  • Lead the product design through 3 development cycles.

Product Design:

  • Wireframes and prototypes using Balsamiq, InVision
  • Low and high fidelity mock-ups using Illustrator or direct to code
  • Coded two iterations of ParseHub Website using CSS/LESS, HTML, JavaScript, Bootstrap and working on top of Python/Flask back-end
  • Developed first design of the ParseHub desktop app by creating color scheme, removing complex features, introducing white space and iterating on the usability of every page and core features.
  • Directly implemented design changes inside code and used Git and GitHub to collaborate with engineers.
  • Create two blog themes - developed in WordPress and Ghost
  • All graphic design on website and in marketing materials - icons, feature examples

Product Management:

  • User research and 100 + hours of usability testing in-person and over screen share.
  • Users flows and journeys for website and desktop app. Processes looked at: signing-up, subscribing and downgrading, on boarding, accomplishing success metrics like creating a project and downloading a data set.
  • Worked with engineers to lead the product through 3 development cycles by understanding the products technical stack, architecture and REST API.
  • Brought the team together to focus on solving core pain points our customers are experiencing. Closely monitors customer complaints and trained customer support on issue reporting.

Cansbridge Fellowship

With 5 other fellows, established a not-for-profit that created a global community of entrepreneurial students and professionals. The Fellowship uses over $100,000 to operate three initiatives:

1) A summer internship program that helps 15 Canadian University students a year find internships in Asia. Students from 25 different Universities worked at companies like CISCO, Daimler, Bosh, venture funds and start-ups.

2) A conference weekend in San Francisco that brings together exceptional students with entrepreneurs, investors and tech professionals.

3) An entrepreneurial community with coaching that accelerates students in their entrepreneurial development and helps them start businesses world-wide.

Results:

  • Applications are growing at 360% year over year with an acceptance rate of 5%.
  • Grew from 1 to 25 schools by creating a new brand, all marketing and digital materials, on-campus recruitment strategy and a tight mentorship program.
  • Created a new selection and interviewing process with 4 rounds that is gearing up to screen 1,000 applicants.
